What this decision means (locked once you confirm)

One identity, three modes. Day and Night Folio are the same components with two ink palettes — building them as a pair keeps them from drifting apart. Lamplight is not a bigger-font toggle on top of the others (your current largeText just inflates the root size); it's a real mode:

  • Type: base ~1.4× with a distance-tuned scale; handwriting (Caveat) is banned — annotations become italic Garamond; weights step up one.
  • Contrast: AAA-target text colors; ink lines thicken; ornament reduced to one gold divider so nothing competes with words.
  • Layout: larger touch targets (one-thumb, phone propped far away), fewer items per screen, roll button huge.

Open question for you — the 20 themes: the Folio identity reads strongest with the ink/paper/gold register shown here. I recommend collapsing to Day + Night + Lamplight as the base modes, keeping the accent hue as the player's choice (Ivy picks teal ink, you pick gold — seals, stamps, quest marks, and the active-nav glow take the hue; paper and leather stay constant). That preserves per-player personality with a fraction of the surface area of 20 full palettes. It changes the "20 color themes" line in CLAUDE.md, so it's your call, not mine.
