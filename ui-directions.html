IGilded Chronicle — the illuminated manuscript, restrained

What changes: engraved gold hairlines with corner flourishes replace generic borders; Cinzel kickers + Garamond italics carry hierarchy; seals/medallions for NPCs; quests become ribbon-marked bands; faint parchment grain adds warmth without skeuomorphic kitsch. Feels like: a rulebook made by a fine press. Risk: ornament discipline — one flourish per surface, or it tips into themed-restaurant.

Recommendation — Direction II, with Direction III as its "artifact layer"

Build Modern Grimoire as the system: it fixes the actual diagnosis (everything currently has identical visual weight — bordered boxes all the way down) with the highest-leverage tools: elevation instead of borders, luminous accent spent only on meaning, typographic hierarchy that already exists in your tokens but was never pushed, and a motion spec. It's the lowest-risk to apply across 30+ components and all 20 themes, because it's a systemization of what exists, not a costume over it.

Then keep the ink: your hand-drawn dice — and a small family of inked artifacts (stamps on quest completion, an inked divider in the Journal, a scrawled margin-note style for player annotations) — live INSIDE the Grimoire as its personality layer. Game moments feel handmade; the chrome feels engineered. That contrast is what premium mobile games actually do. Direction I's gold flourishes survive as the level-up / critical-hit ceremony treatment only.
